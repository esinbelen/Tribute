İsmet İnönü

İsmet İnönü and the Great Leader of Turkish Republic, Atatürk, are staring each other.

  • 1884 - born in İzmir, Turkey
  • 1894 - completed his primary education in Sivas and graduated Sivas Military Junior High School
  • 1903 - graduated from the Imperial School of Military Engineering as gunnery officer, and received his first military assignment in the Ottoman Army
  • 1912 - he was in the Commander-in-Chief Headquarters 1st Branch of the Ministry of War for a year and was appointed as the Commander of the Çatalca Army Right Wing during the Second Balkan War. After the war, he joined the delegation negotiating with the Bulgarians in signing the Istanbul Treaty as a military advisor
  • 1914 - he played an active role in the movement of reforming the army initiated by Enver Pasha, who was appointed as the Minister of War and Chief of War of the Military
  • 1915 - During World War I, he worked with Mustafa Kemal Pasha as the Corps Commander in the Caucasian Front
  • 1917 - Appointed to the 4th Army Corps Command. Fought in Halep, Palestinian Front
  • 1918 - Appointed to the Undersecretariat of the Ministry of War
  • 1919 - Became a military consultant in the commission established to prepare for the Paris Peace Conference
  • 1920 - He returned to Ankara upon the call of Mustafa Kemal Pasha and broke all official ties with Istanbul. He joined to Grand National Assembly of Turkey as Edirne deputy
